# Module 4: Closing Process Checklist

## 4.2 The Closing Process: A Step-by-Step Guide

Once you have a signed Purchase and Sale Agreement with the seller and a signed Assignment of Contract with your cash buyer, it's time to hand things over to your title company or closing attorney.

This enhanced checklist includes both standard closings and advanced creative financing structures discovered through real transaction analysis.

---

### **Phase 1: Opening Escrow**

*   [ ] **Choose a Wholesaler-Friendly Title Company or Attorney:** Not all title companies are comfortable with assignments or double closes. Find one that is experienced with investor transactions and creative financing.
*   [ ] **Send Over the Executed Contracts:**
    *   Email the fully signed **Purchase and Sale Agreement** to your chosen title company/attorney.
    *   Email the fully signed **Assignment of Contract** to them as well.
    *   Include any **Subject-To Addendums**, **Trust Documents**, or **Seller Finance Agreements** if applicable.
*   [ ] **Open Escrow:** The title company will officially open an escrow file for the transaction.
*   [ ] **Deliver the Earnest Money Deposits:**
    *   Ensure your (the wholesaler's) EMD is deposited with the title company as specified in the Purchase and Sale Agreement.
    *   Ensure your cash buyer's EMD is deposited with the title company as specified in the Assignment of Contract.
*   [ ] **Attorney Review:** Have real estate attorney review all documents for creative financing compliance.

---

### **Phase 2: The Title Search and Closing Prep**

*   [ ] **The Title Company Conducts a Title Search:** They will search public records to ensure the seller has the legal right to sell the property and that there are no other liens or encumbrances on the title (e.g., old mortgages, tax liens).
*   [ ] **Review the Title Commitment:** The title company will issue a title commitment that lists any issues that need to be cleared before closing. Review this with your title officer.
*   [ ] **Coordinate with All Parties:** Stay in communication with the seller, the buyer, and the title company to ensure everyone is on the same page.
*   [ ] **Schedule the Closing:** The title company will coordinate with all parties to schedule a closing date and time.
*   [ ] **Creative Financing Document Review:** For subject-to, trust, or seller finance deals, ensure all legal documents are properly prepared and recorded.
*   [ ] **Attorney Final Review:** Have attorney review all closing documents for compliance.

---

### **⚠️ WIRE FRAUD PREVENTION — READ BEFORE EVERY CLOSING**

Wire fraud is the #1 real estate scam. Criminals intercept email communications and send fake wire instructions. One wrong wire can cost you or your buyer tens of thousands of dollars — and it's nearly impossible to recover.

**Mandatory Protocol:**
1. **Never trust wire instructions sent via email** — even if they appear to come from the title company
2. **Always call the title company directly** at a phone number you looked up independently (not from the email)
3. **Verbally confirm** the account number, routing number, and bank name before wiring
4. **If wire instructions change at any point** — treat it as fraud until proven otherwise
5. **Send a small test wire** ($10) first on large transactions, confirm receipt before sending the balance

**Red Flags:**
- Wire instructions arrive via email at the last minute
- Instructions are different from what was previously discussed
- Pressure to wire immediately without time to verify
- Email address is slightly different from the title company's normal address

---

### **Phase 3: Closing Day**

*   [ ] **The Buyer Wires Funds:** Your cash buyer will wire the remaining balance of the purchase price to the title company. **Verify wire instructions by phone before sending** (see Wire Fraud Prevention above).
*   [ ] **The Seller Signs Closing Documents:** The seller will sign the deed and other documents to transfer ownership.
*   [ ] **The Buyer Signs Closing Documents:** The buyer will sign their side of the paperwork.
*   [ ] **Creative Financing Document Execution:** For subject-to, trust, or seller finance deals, ensure all legal documents are properly signed and notarized.
*   [ ] **The Transaction is Funded and Recorded:** The title company will disburse all funds, including:
    *   Paying off the seller's mortgage (if any).
    *   Paying the seller their net proceeds.
    *   **Paying you your assignment fee!**
    *   The new deed is recorded with the county, officially transferring ownership to your buyer.
*   [ ] **Performance Mortgage Recording:** For subject-to deals, ensure performance mortgage is properly recorded.
*   [ ] **Leaseback Agreement Execution:** If seller needs to remain after closing, execute leaseback agreement.

---

### **Phase 4: Creative Financing Specific Steps**

*   [ ] **Subject-To Mortgage Transfer:** Ensure existing mortgage payments are properly transferred to buyer.
*   [ ] **Trust Document Recording:** For trust acquisitions, ensure trust documents are properly recorded.
*   [ ] **Performance Mortgage Recording:** Record performance mortgage for subject-to security.
*   [ ] **Leaseback Agreement Execution:** If seller needs to remain, execute leaseback agreement.
*   [ ] **Insurance Transfer:** Transfer homeowner's insurance to new owner.
*   [ ] **Property Tax Notification:** Notify tax authorities of ownership change.

### **Post-Closing**

*   [ ] **Confirm You Received Your Wire:** Double-check your bank account to ensure you've received your assignment fee.
*   [ ] **Thank All Parties:** A quick thank you to the seller, buyer, and title company goes a long way in building relationships for future deals.
*   [ ] **Ask for a Testimonial:** If your cash buyer is happy with the deal, ask for a testimonial.
*   [ ] **Document Filing:** File all closing documents for your records.
*   [ ] **Follow-up Communication:** Check in with all parties 1 week after closing.
*   [ ] **Relationship Building:** Maintain contact for future opportunities.
*   [ ] **Write Agent Lesson:** Document what worked, what didn't, and key objections handled in `agent-lessons/YYYY-MM-DD-[address].md`

### **🚨 If the Deal Falls Apart**

Deals fall through. Here's the protocol:

**If the Buyer Backs Out:**
1. Check the Assignment Agreement for default provisions
2. Determine if the buyer's EMD is forfeitable (usually yes if they back out without cause)
3. Activate your backup buyer immediately
4. If no backup buyer, re-market the deal — you still have the property under contract
5. If you can't find a new buyer before your contract expires, use your inspection contingency to cancel

**If the Seller Backs Out:**
1. Review the Purchase & Sale Agreement for seller default remedies
2. Options: (a) demand specific performance, (b) sue for damages, (c) accept the situation and move on
3. In practice, most wholesalers accept the situation — suing a seller is expensive and damages your reputation
4. Document everything in writing

**If Title Issues Are Found:**
1. Determine if the issue is workable (lien payoff at closing) or a deal-killer (active bankruptcy, clouded title)
2. For workable issues: negotiate with seller to resolve at closing
3. For deal-killers: cancel the contract using your inspection contingency, recover your EMD

**Releasing Earnest Money:**
- Both parties must sign a mutual release for the title company to disburse EMD
- If there's a dispute, the title company holds the funds until resolved
- Most disputes settle without litigation — be reasonable
