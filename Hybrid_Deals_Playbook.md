# Module 3: Hybrid Deals Playbook

## 3.1 What is a Hybrid Deal?

A hybrid deal, as the name suggests, is a creative financing strategy that combines two or more different deal structures into a single transaction. This strategy is most often used when a seller has equity in their property but not enough for a traditional cash offer to make sense, and they are not in a position to fully finance the deal themselves.

The most common hybrid structure combines:
*   **A small amount of cash at closing** to the seller.
*   **A seller financing agreement** for the remaining portion of the equity.
*   **Taking over the seller's existing mortgage** (a "Subject To" transaction).

**When to Propose a Hybrid Deal:**
*   The seller has some equity, but not a lot.
*   The seller needs some cash at closing, but doesn't need all of their equity at once.
*   The property has an existing mortgage with a favorable interest rate.
*   A traditional cash offer would be too low to be accepted.

## 3.2 Structuring a Hybrid Deal: An Example

Let's say you are looking at a property with the following numbers:

*   **After-Repair Value (ARV):** $300,000
*   **Existing Mortgage Balance:** $200,000
*   **Seller's Equity:** $100,000
*   **Seller's Need:** The seller needs $20,000 in cash at closing to pay off some debts.

A traditional cash offer would be too low (around $210,000 based on the 70% rule), which would not even cover the seller's mortgage.

**The Hybrid Solution:**
1.  **You agree to a purchase price of $250,000.** This is much higher than a cash offer and is more likely to be accepted.
2.  **You give the seller $20,000 in cash at closing.** This meets their immediate need.
3.  **You take over the existing mortgage of $200,000** ("Subject To").
4.  **The remaining $30,000 is structured as a seller-financed note.** You will pay the seller this amount over an agreed-upon term with interest.

**The Win-Win:**
*   **The Seller:** Gets the debt relief they need, a fair price for their property, and monthly income from the seller-financed note.
*   **The Wholesaler/Investor:** Gets into a property with a high ARV for a small amount of cash out of pocket.

## 3.3 Pitching the Hybrid Deal

The key to pitching a hybrid deal is to present it as a solution to the seller's problem.

1.  **Start with the Cash Offer:** Always present your (low) cash offer first. When the seller inevitably balks at the price, it opens the door for you to present a more creative solution.
2.  **Understand Their Needs:** Ask questions to understand *why* they are selling and what they hope to accomplish. "If I could get you your full asking price, but it wasn't all in cash, would that be something you'd be open to discussing?"
3.  **Explain the Benefits:** Frame the hybrid deal in terms of how it benefits them.
    *   "You get the cash you need now."
    *   "You get a fair price for your home."
    *   "You get to be the bank and earn monthly income."
4.  **Keep it Simple:** Don't get bogged down in technical jargon. Use analogies. "It's like you're selling me the house, but instead of the bank paying you, I am."

## 3.4 Legal and Paperwork

A hybrid deal requires more complex paperwork than a standard cash deal. You will need:
*   A **Purchase and Sale Agreement** that clearly outlines all three components of the deal.
*   A **Promissory Note** for the seller-financed portion.
*   A **Deed of Trust** or **Mortgage** to secure the seller-financed note.
*   A **Subject To Addendum** for the existing mortgage.

**It is highly recommended that you work with a real estate attorney who is experienced in creative financing to draft and review all documents.**
