# Understanding and Working with Your Title Company

## The Critical Role of a Title Company in Wholesaling

In a real estate transaction, the title company (or in some states, a closing attorney) acts as a neutral third party. Their primary job is to ensure that the seller has the legal right to sell the property and to facilitate the transfer of ownership to the buyer. For a wholesaler, the title company is one of the most important members of your team. A good, "wholesaler-friendly" title company can make your deals run smoothly, while an inexperienced one can kill your deals.

---

## Glossary of Essential Title Vocabulary

*   **Title Search:** A thorough examination of public records to determine the legal ownership of a property and to uncover any claims or liens against it.
*   **Title Insurance:** An insurance policy that protects the new owner (your cash buyer) and the lender from any financial loss caused by defects in the title.
*   **Escrow:** A neutral third-party account where all funds, documents, and other assets related to the transaction are held until the closing is complete.
*   **Cloud on Title:** Any issue that arises from a title search that casts doubt on the seller's legal ability to transfer ownership. Examples include unpaid taxes, old mortgage liens, or a contractor's lien.
*   **HUD-1/Closing Disclosure (CD):** A standardized document that itemizes all charges and credits to the buyer and seller in a real estate transaction. As a wholesaler, your assignment fee will appear on this statement.
*   **Lien:** A legal claim against a property for the payment of a debt.
*   **Encumbrance:** Any claim against a property, such as a mortgage, lien, or restrictive covenant.

## Checklist: What Your Title Company Needs from You

To initiate and complete a closing, your title company will need the following:

*   [ ] **Fully Executed Purchase and Sale Agreement:** The complete, signed contract between you and the seller.
*   [ ] **Fully Executed Assignment of Contract:** The complete, signed contract between you and your cash buyer.
*   [ ] **Contact Information for All Parties:**
    *   [ ] Seller's Full Name, Phone Number, and Email Address
    *   [ ] Buyer's Full Name, Phone Number, and Email Address
    *   [ ] Your Full Name, Phone Number, and Email Address
*   [ ] **Information on Existing Mortgages:** If the seller has an existing mortgage, the title company will need the lender's name and the loan number to order a payoff statement.
*   [ ] **HOA Information:** If the property is in a Homeowners Association, the title company will need the name and contact information for the HOA to verify any outstanding dues.
*   [ ] **Your Wiring Instructions:** The bank account information where you want to receive your assignment fee.

## Finding and Vetting a "Wholesaler-Friendly" Title Company

Not all title companies are created equal. Many are not familiar or comfortable with wholesale transactions, especially assignments of contract.

### How to Find Them:

1.  **Ask for Referrals:** The best way is to get a referral from an experienced local investor or another wholesaler.
2.  **Attend Local REIA Meetings:** Network with other investors and ask them which title companies they use.
3.  **Call and Ask:** Call local title companies and ask to speak with an escrow officer or attorney.

### How to Vet Them (Key Questions to Ask):

*   **"Are you familiar with and do you handle assignments of contract?"** This is the most important question. If they hesitate or say no, move on.
*   **"Do you have experience working with real estate investors?"** You want a company that understands the speed and nuances of investor transactions.
*   **"Are you able to perform a double closing if necessary?"** A double close is another common exit strategy for wholesalers, and you want a title company that can handle both.
*   **"What are your standard fees for a wholesale transaction?"**

### Building a Strong Relationship:

*   **Be Professional:** Provide all necessary documents promptly and be responsive to their requests.
*   **Bring Them Business:** The more deals you bring to a title company, the more they will value your business.
*   **Show Your Gratitude:** A simple "thank you" after a smooth closing goes a long way.

A great title company is a partner in your success. Take the time to find a good one, and it will pay dividends in your wholesaling business.
