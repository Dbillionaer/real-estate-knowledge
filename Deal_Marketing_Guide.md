# Module 5: Deal Marketing Guide

## 5.2 Marketing Your Deal to Cash Buyers

Once you have a property under contract, it's time to market it to your cash buyers list. The goal is to provide them with all the information they need to make a quick decision.

**Remember:** You are not selling a house. You are selling a *deal*. Your marketing should focus on the numbers and the investment potential.

---

### **Step 1: Create a Property Package**

A property package is a concise summary of the deal that you will send to your buyers. It should be professional and easy to read.

**What to Include:**

*   **Headline:** A catchy headline that grabs their attention (e.g., "Hot Deal in [Neighborhood]! 3/2 with Huge Upside").
*   **Property Details:**
    *   Address
    *   Bed/Bath Count
    *   Square Footage
    *   Year Built
*   **The Numbers (The Most Important Part):**
    *   Your Asking Price (the price of your contract + your assignment fee)
    *   After-Repair Value (ARV)
    *   Estimated Repair Costs
    *   Estimated Profit for the Buyer
*   **Photos and Videos:**
    *   Take clear, well-lit photos of every room, as well as the exterior and any areas that need repair.
    *   A video walkthrough is even better.
*   **Access Information:** How the buyer can see the property (e.g., lockbox code, scheduled showing times).
*   **Your Contact Information:** Make it easy for them to reach you.

---

### **Step 2: Send it to Your Buyers List**

The best way to send your deals is via email.

**Best Practices:**

*   **Use an Email Marketing Service:** Services like Mailchimp or Constant Contact allow you to manage your list and send professional-looking emails.
*   **Segment Your List:** As you get to know your buyers, you can segment your list based on their buying criteria. This allows you to send targeted deals to the buyers who are most likely to be interested.
*   **The "Teaser" Email:**
    *   Your initial email should be a "teaser" with the high-level details of the deal.
    *   **Subject Line:** Make it compelling (e.g., "[Your Company] - New Deal - [Property Address]").
    *   **Body:** Include the headline, the numbers, and a few of the best photos.
    *   **Call to Action:** "Reply to this email for more photos and the full property package."
*   **Create a Sense of Urgency:** Let your buyers know that you are sending the deal to your entire list and that you expect it to go fast.

---

### **Step 3: Handling Responses and Showings**

*   **Be Responsive:** When buyers reply to your email, be ready to send them the full property package and answer their questions immediately.
*   **Schedule a Showing:** The most serious buyers will want to see the property. It's often easiest to schedule a single "open house" for all interested buyers to come at the same time.
*   **Collect Offers:** As offers come in, be prepared to negotiate. The first offer is not always the best.
*   **Get it in Writing:** Once you agree on a price and terms with a buyer, get it in writing with an **Assignment of Contract** and collect their non-refundable earnest money deposit.

By following this process, you will be able to market your deals effectively, find a buyer quickly, and build a reputation as a reliable source of profitable investment properties.
