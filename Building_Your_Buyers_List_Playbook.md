# Module 5: Building Your Buyers List Playbook

## 5.1 What is a Cash Buyer?

A cash buyer is a real estate investor who has the funds available to purchase a property without needing a traditional bank loan. These are the lifeblood of a wholesaling business.

**Why are Cash Buyers Essential?**
*   **Speed:** They can close quickly, which is a major selling point for motivated sellers.
*   **Certainty:** There is no risk of a loan falling through.
*   **"As-Is" Purchases:** They are accustomed to buying properties that need work.

Your goal is not just to find any cash buyer, but to build a list of *reliable, repeat* buyers who you can count on to close.

## 5.2 Strategies for Finding Cash Buyers

### Public Records
*   **Concept:** Search county public records for recent cash sales of properties that are similar to the ones you plan to wholesale.
*   **Process:**
    1.  Go to your county recorder's or property appraiser's website.
    2.  Search for recent sales (last 3-6 months) in your target zip codes.
    3.  Look for transactions where the "buyer" is an LLC or a known investor and there is no mortgage recorded.
    4.  Skip trace the LLC or individual to get their contact information.
    5.  Call them and ask if they are looking to purchase more investment properties in the area.

### Real Estate Auctions
*   **Concept:** Attend local real estate auctions (both in-person and online) to see who is buying.
*   **Process:**
    1.  Find upcoming foreclosure or tax-deed auctions in your area.
    2.  The list of registered bidders is often public. These are all cash-ready investors.
    3.  Attend the auction and network with the bidders and winners.

### Networking
*   **Real Estate Investment Associations (REIAs):** Attend local REIA meetings. This is one of the best places to meet other investors, including cash buyers.
*   **"For Rent" Signs:** Call the numbers on "For Rent" signs in your target neighborhoods. The owner of a rental property is an investor, and they may be looking to buy more.
*   **Craigslist:** Look in the "Real Estate for Sale" section for ads that say "fixer-upper" or "investor special." The people posting these ads are often other wholesalers or investors who may be cash buyers themselves.

### Online Marketing
*   **Facebook Groups:** Join local real estate investing Facebook groups and post about your deals.
*   **LinkedIn:** Connect with individuals whose job titles include "real estate investor."
*   **BiggerPockets:** The forums on BiggerPockets are a great place to connect with investors from all over the country.

## 5.3 Vetting Your Buyers

Not all cash buyers are created equal. You need to vet them to ensure they are serious and have the ability to close.

**Key Questions to Ask:**
*   **"What are you looking for?"** (e.g., zip codes, property types, level of rehab). This helps you send them only the deals that fit their criteria.
*   **"How are you funding your purchases?"** You want to hear that they are using their own cash, a line of credit, or a hard money loan.
*   **"Can you provide proof of funds?"** A serious buyer will have no problem sending you a recent bank statement or a letter from their hard money lender.
*   **"How quickly can you close?"** A good cash buyer can typically close in 7-14 days.

By building a strong, well-vetted buyers list, you will be able to sell your deals faster and with more certainty, which is the key to scaling your wholesaling business.
