# Agent Lesson: 456 Pine Ave (Deal Fell Through)

## Deal Summary
Distressed property, seller seemed motivated initially. 2/1 bungalow, major repairs needed.

## What Happened
1. Seller initially agreed to $72K offer
2. Contract signed, earnest money deposited
3. Title search revealed $18K IRS tax lien
4. Seller couldn't pay lien at closing
5. Deal cancelled, earnest money returned

## What Went Wrong
- Did not pre-screen for title issues before making offer
- Seller was unaware of the tax lien
- No contingency plan for lien payoff

## What Could Improve
- Ask about known liens during initial seller call
- Run preliminary title check before contract (cheap online services)
- Build relationships with title companies for quick pre-lim searches

## Key Takeaway
**Title issues kill deals.** Always ask sellers about liens, judgments, and mortgages upfront. A $50 preliminary title check before contract could have saved 12 days of work.

## Red Flags Missed
- Seller was evasive about "why they needed to sell quickly"
- Property had recent refinance (should have asked about loan amount)

## Time Invested (Lost)
- Initial call: 30 min
- Property research: 1 hour
- Offer preparation: 30 min
- Title coordination: 2 hours
- Deal cancellation: 1 hour
**Total: ~5 hours**
