# Agent Lesson: 555 Cedar Ct (Partial Success - Reduced Fee)

## Deal Summary
Initially contracted at $95K with $12K assignment fee. After buyer inspection discovered mold, deal renegotiated. Still closed but at reduced fee.

## What Happened
1. Contracted at $95K, marketed to buyers
2. Buyer #1 accepted at $107K ($12K fee)
3. Inspection revealed mold in basement ($15K remediation)
4. Buyer #1 backed out
5. Re-marketed with disclosure
6. Buyer #2 accepted at $105,500 ($7.5K fee)
7. Closed 2 weeks later than planned

## What Went Wrong
- No pre-marketing inspection done
- Mold was visible but we missed it during walkthrough
- Should have tested for mold (older home, musty smell)
- First buyer was strong, lost them over issue we should have caught

## Financial Impact
| Scenario | Fee | Notes |
|----------|-----|-------|
| Original deal | $12,000 | Buyer #1 |
| Actual close | $7,500 | Buyer #2, reduced fee |
| **Lost revenue** | **$4,500** | 37.5% reduction |

## What Could Improve
- **Always inspect before marketing** - $400 inspection saves thousands
- **Trust your nose** - musty smell = potential mold
- **Have backup buyers ready** - reduced our renegotiation power
- **Build inspection contingency into timeline** - 7-day inspection period

## Key Takeaway
**$400 inspection beats $4,500 fee reduction.** Pre-marketing inspections are non-negotiable for professional wholesalers. Make it part of your standard process.

## Process Change Implemented
After this deal, we now require:
1. Initial walkthrough with moisture meter
2. Professional inspection within 48 hours of contract
3. Full disclosure to all potential buyers
4. Price adjustment if major issues found

## Silver Lining
- Still closed the deal (many would have died completely)
- Buyer #2 is now a repeat customer
- Implemented better inspection process
- Seller was happy we didn't cancel
