# Agent Lesson: 321 Maple Ln (Subject-To Success)

## Deal Summary
Seller had 3.5% mortgage with $145K balance. Property worth $285K ARV. Didn't want to sell traditionally due to tax implications. Subject-to was perfect solution.

## What Went Well
- Identified subject-to opportunity immediately (low interest rate, motivated seller)
- Used subject_to_playbook - pitched "take over payments" concept
- Seller understood benefits: no foreclosure, credit preserved, debt relief
- Found buyer who wanted the low-rate loan assumption
- Structured: $145K loan takeover + $18K assignment fee

## Deal Structure
```
Original Loan: $145,000 @ 3.5% (monthly payment: $651)
Assignment Fee: $18,000 (paid to us by end buyer)
Buyer's Total: $18,000 down + assumes $651/month payment
ARV After Repairs: $285,000
Equity Position: ~$100K for end buyer
```

## What Could Improve
- Took 5 weeks to close (subject-to documentation is complex)
- Should have had subject-to attorney on standby
- Insurance transfer was complicated - needed proper trust setup

## Key Takeaway
**Subject-to deals require specialized knowledge.** Have your attorney prepare subject-to documents in advance. Insurance is the trickiest part - use land trust to avoid due-on-sale clause triggers.

## Subject-To vs Cash Comparison
| Scenario | Seller Net | Our Fee | Buyer Benefit |
|----------|------------|---------|---------------|
| Cash offer | $120K | $12K | Full ownership |
| Subject-to | Debt relief + $5K | $18K | Low-rate financing |

Seller chose subject-to because they couldn't afford cash-out and wanted credit preserved.
