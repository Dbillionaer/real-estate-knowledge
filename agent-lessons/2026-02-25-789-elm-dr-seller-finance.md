# Agent Lesson: 789 Elm Dr (Seller Finance Success)

## Deal Summary
Free-and-clear property owned by retired couple. They wanted monthly income, not lump sum. Seller finance was perfect fit.

## What Went Well
- Recognized seller finance opportunity early (free-and-clear = no mortgage)
- Used seller_finance_playbook - pitched monthly income vs lump sum
- Seller was sophisticated - understood the benefit
- Structured deal: $95K purchase, $10K down, $85K financed at 5% for 30 years
- Found buyer who wanted rental with seller financing

## Negotiation Details
- Initial ask: $145K cash
- Our first pitch: $85K with $15K down, 6% interest
- Final agreement: $95K with $10K down, 5% interest, 30-year term
- Assignment fee: $15K (higher than cash deal because of complexity)

## What Could Improve
- Took 4 calls to explain seller financing concept
- Should have had visual one-pager ready to email
- Need attorney review of seller finance documents (did it, but should do earlier)

## Key Takeaway
**Listen for seller finance signals.** When sellers mention "monthly income" or "don't need all cash now" - pivot to seller finance immediately. Higher fees, happier sellers.

| Cash offer | $80K lump sum | $10K |
| Seller finance | $10K down + $456/mo for 30 yrs | $15K |
