# Module 4: Contract Walkthroughs Guide

## 4.1 Introduction to Contracts

Your contracts are the legal backbone of your wholesaling business. They must be clear, concise, and legally sound. While the templates provided in the `contracts` folder are a great starting point, you should **always have a qualified real estate attorney review and approve your contracts to ensure they comply with your state's laws.**

This enhanced guide covers both standard contracts and advanced creative financing documents discovered through real transaction analysis.

### **The Purchase and Sale Agreement**

This is the core document for any deal. It's the agreement between you (the buyer) and the property owner (the seller).

**Key Clauses to Understand:**

*   **Parties:** Clearly identifies the Buyer and Seller. Your name or your business entity's name will be the Buyer.
*   **Property Description:** The full legal address and Parcel ID of the property.
*   **Purchase Price:** The total amount you have agreed to pay for the property.
*   **Earnest Money Deposit (EMD):** The amount of your deposit and where it will be held (e.g., with a title company). A smaller EMD (e.g., $100) is common in wholesale deals.
*   **Contingencies:** These are your "escape clauses."
    *   **Inspection/Due Diligence Period:** This is the most important contingency for a wholesaler. It gives you a set amount of time (e.g., 14-30 days) to inspect the property and decide if it's a good deal. If not, you can cancel the contract and get your EMD back.
*   **Assignability Clause:** This clause is **non-negotiable** for a wholesaler. It must clearly state that the buyer has the right to assign the contract to another party. A common phrasing is: *"Buyer may assign this contract without the prior written consent of Seller."*
*   **Closing Date:** The date by which the transaction must be finalized.

---

### **The Assignment of Contract**

This is the simple, one-page document you sign with your cash buyer. It transfers all your rights and obligations under the Purchase and Sale Agreement to them in exchange for your assignment fee.

**Key Clauses:**

*   **Parties:** Identifies the "Assignor" (you) and the "Assignee" (your cash buyer).
*   **Original Contract:** References the original Purchase and Sale Agreement by date and parties.
*   **Assignment Fee:** Clearly states the amount your cash buyer is paying you for the assignment.
*   **Non-Refundable Deposit:** Your cash buyer should give you a non-refundable deposit when they sign the assignment. This protects you if they back out of the deal.

---

### **The Joint Venture (JV) Agreement**

This contract is used when you partner with another wholesaler on a deal.

**Common Scenarios:**
*   One partner brings the deal (the property under contract).
*   The other partner brings the cash buyer.

**Key Clauses:**
*   **Roles and Responsibilities:** Clearly defines who is responsible for what.
*   **Profit Split:** How the total assignment fee will be divided between the partners (typically 50/50).
*   **Confidentiality:** Prevents one partner from going around the other to steal the deal or the buyer.

---

### **Addendums and Amendments**

*   **Addendum:** A document that adds new terms to the original contract (e.g., a "Subject To" addendum).
*   **Amendment:** A document that changes existing terms in the original contract (e.g., extending the closing date).

Both must be signed by the original buyer and seller to be legally binding.

## 4.3 Advanced Creative Financing Contracts

### **Subject-To Addendum**
**Purpose**: Legal document for taking over seller's existing mortgage
**Key Clauses:**
- **Mortgage Assumption**: Buyer agrees to make mortgage payments
- **Deed Transfer**: Property title transfers to buyer
- **Performance Mortgage**: Security for seller if buyer defaults
- **Due-on-Sale Clause**: Acknowledgment of potential lender enforcement

**From Transcript Analysis:**
```
"Property is being sold subject to existing mortgage of $140,000.
Buyer agrees to make all mortgage payments on time.
If buyer misses 2 consecutive payments, property reverts to seller."
```

### **Trust Acquisition Documents**
**Purpose**: Transfer property through trust structure to avoid due-on-sale issues
**Key Documents:**
- **Trust Agreement**: Establishes trust entity
- **Deed Transfer**: Property transferred into trust
- **Interest Purchase Agreement**: Buyer acquires controlling interest
- **Garn-St. Germain Compliance**: Legal foundation for trust transfer

**From Transcript Analysis:**
```
"Trust created under Garn-St. Germain Act of 1981.
Property transferred into trust, buyer purchases controlling interest.
No public record of transaction, complete privacy protection."
```

### **Seller Finance Documents**
**Purpose**: Seller acts as lender for property purchase
**Key Documents:**
- **Promissory Note**: Buyer's promise to pay specified amount
- **Deed of Trust**: Property as collateral for loan
- **Balloon Payment Provision**: Large payment at end of term
- **Amortization Schedule**: Payment breakdown over time

**From Transcript Analysis:**
```
"Promissory note for $205,000 at 1.5% interest.
Monthly payments of $707 for 60 months.
Balloon payment of $176,000 at term end.
Property serves as collateral for loan."
```

### **Performance Mortgage**
**Purpose**: Additional security for seller in subject-to transactions
**Key Clauses:**
- **Payment Guarantee**: Legal requirement for buyer to make payments
- **Default Remedies**: Automatic property reversion on missed payments
- **Recording**: Document recorded with county for legal enforcement
- **Term**: Typically 2-3 months missed payments trigger default

**From Transcript Analysis:**
```
"Performance mortgage recorded with county.
If buyer misses 2 consecutive payments, property automatically reverts to seller.
Provides additional security beyond standard subject-to structure."
```

### **Deed in Lieu of Foreclosure**
**Purpose**: Alternative to formal foreclosure process
**Key Clauses:**
- **Voluntary Transfer**: Seller agrees to transfer property back to buyer
- **No Foreclosure**: Avoids formal foreclosure proceedings
- **Condition**: Property returned in same or better condition
- **Timeline**: Immediate transfer upon default

**From Transcript Analysis:**
```
"Deed in lieu of foreclosure executed at closing.
If buyer defaults on payments, property transfers back to seller immediately.
Avoids 3-6 month foreclosure process, completed in days."
```

### **Leaseback Agreement**
**Purpose**: Allows seller to remain in property after closing
**Key Clauses:**
- **Lease Term**: Specific rental period (30-90 days typical)
- **Monthly Rent**: Fair market rent for property
- **Security Deposit**: Protection for property damage
- **Move-Out Date**: Specific date seller must vacate

**From Transcript Analysis:**
```
"Seller leaseback for 30 days at $2,000/month.
Security deposit of $2,000 required.
Property must be returned in same condition, normal wear excepted."
```

## 4.4 Multi-Property and Portfolio Contracts

### **Blanket Mortgage Addendum**
**Purpose**: Handle mortgages covering multiple properties
**Key Clauses:**
- **Property Allocation**: Fair distribution of mortgage across properties
- **Payment Structure**: Per-property payment allocation
- **Release Provisions**: Process for releasing individual properties
- **Default Impact**: How default affects entire portfolio

**From Transcript Analysis:**
```
"Blanket mortgage of $2.8M covers 26 properties.
Per-property allocation: $107.7k.
Individual property sales require mortgage division approval."
```

### **Portfolio Acquisition Agreement**
**Purpose**: Structure acquisition of multiple properties
**Key Clauses:**
- **Property List**: Complete list of all properties included
- **Package Pricing**: Bulk discount for multiple properties
- **Individual Closings**: Separate closing for each property
- **Commission Structure**: Agent compensation across portfolio

**From Transcript Analysis:**
```
"Portfolio of 26 properties acquired as package.
5 properties initially, remaining 21 referred to network.
Bulk discount of 5-10% for package acquisition."
```
